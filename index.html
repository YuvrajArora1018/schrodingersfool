I’m Yuvraj Arora -- high school IBDP student at Suncity School International Academia, Schrödinger’s Fool (simultaneously making sense and not), and someone who’s way too curious about how and why anything exists at all. I’m into quantum physics and cosmology, especially the parts where equations stop behaving and reality starts glitching.


My research interests are split across two fronts: understanding the role of quantum coherence in foundational physics, and figuring out whether Dark Matter is actually a thing or just a placeholder for our ignorance.


I’m also one of the rare string theory non-believers; not because I hate elegance, but because 10 dimensions and no testable predictions (yet) feels like a WattPad fanfiction. That said, I do have a soft spot for Loop Quantum Gravity; quantized spacetime just feels more grounded. As for quantum interpretations, I lean toward the Many-Worlds Interpretation because if there’s an alternate universe where Chelsea wins consistently, I’d like to believe in it.


Outside the world of physics, I support Chelsea through all the emotional trauma, believe Darth Vader did nothing wrong (objectively true (fight me -- in the politest manner)), and think Mega Charizard X could single-handedly defeat every living thing. An Investigation on Quantum Decoherence as a Cause of Emergent Gravitational Effects
Proposed a high-energy beamline experiment at CERN to test whether quantum decoherence in materials could mimic gravitational anomalies, potentially offering an alternative to dark matter explanations. Explored theoretical links between quantum information loss and emergent gravity models (e.g., Verlinde’s theory) within an experimental physics framework. Currently in consideration at the Beamline For Schools competition. Click to view.
